PROJECT 3

COLLECTION EVALUATION PLAN

COMPETENCY:

  • Application of management principles and interdisciplinary best practices in library and information agency environments.

Description

I completed a Collection Evaluation Plan paper for my Collection and Access Management class (ISLT 7313, Spring 2024). It consisted of selecting a library, a collection evaluation focus, and an outline of approach including the evaluation and selection of appropriate methods covered throughout the course. I selected the UMKC Miller Nichols Library for an evaluation of the relevance and use of their LGBTQ+ fiction materials. I chose to determine the relevance of the collection through the imprint date of materials, formats available, reading level, the presence of popular or recommended texts, and their corresponding circulation statistics, and to combine that data with user perceptions which could be used in order to improve the searchability, relevance, and use of the collection as well as plan for future selection in the area.

Reflection

This was one of my few papers with a basis solely in methods covered in class rather than being based in research, and as such required a slightly different approach than I am accustomed to. It gave me an opportunity to consider how to plan and manage a project, including the comparison and selection of appropriate techniques. I was able to apply some of the skills I learned at work in designing surveys, with an emphasis on user anonymity and brevity to increase completion rate. If I were to do this project again it would be nice to try actually carrying out a small scale evaluation, in order to get more hands-on project management experience.
