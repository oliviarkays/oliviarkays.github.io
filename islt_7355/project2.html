PROJECT 2

VIDEO GAME USER PREFERENCE & DISCOVERY SYSTEMS RESEARCH PAPER & PRESENTATION

COMPETENCIES:

  • Application of the core principles, ethics, values, and body of knowledge to questions in library and information science, according to area of specialty.
  • Competency in information organization and with understanding and evaluating information and communication technologies relevant to the information professions.
  • Assessment of community needs and formulation of plans to respond to users of information agencies.

Description

I completed a research paper and presentation for the final project of my class on Finding and Recommending Popular Culture Sources (ISLT 9410, Spring 2024). It consisted of a literature review of research on video game user preference for challenge types, an analysis of two existing video game discovery and recommendation systems for the representation of challenge types, and suggestions for improvement to these discovery systems. Through the literature review I found that challenge types are currently underrepresented in user preference research but are a growing focus as the field moves away from user profiles toward more well-rounded approaches that incorporate challenge type preferences, trait models, and mental models. The analysis of the existing game discovery systems resulted in suggestions for improvement through adaptations following mental model frameworks in order to address the game aspects most influential to selection and satisfaction, such as challenge type or learning curve.

Reflection

The research paper is one of my strongest thus far, with decent structure, concrete recommendations for the improvement of discovery systems, and several avenues for future research. If I were to do this project again I would have begun work on the paper earlier in order to finish it before the presentation, which was due first but required a summary of the research paper.
